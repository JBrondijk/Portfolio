DRP

Design for Social Innovation:

During this elective, I was introduced to the concept of transformative practices. I read a lot of papers about the topic, which we had to summarize in short infographics. Doing this every week trained my skills in reading and summarizing papers.

As for the topic of the elective, I learnt a lot about strategies I could apply in my design processes, such as the first, second and third person perspectives and how switching between the three can lead to new insights in your designs.

B&E

A Designerly Perspective on IOT:

A growing systems approach

During this elective I learned to look at the world through a new lens. The lens of affordances. While this is mostly useful in designing physical interactions, games, and especially VR games, also heavily use affordances from real life to communicate potential interactions. In my FMP project I analyzed current interactive education methods through the lens of affordances, and found that the most successful designs were the ones with the most obvious affordances.

T&R

Community Xperience Design:

During this elective, I learned a lot about how to manage online communities, and how to use existing online communities to find design opportunities. We also used online platforms, like amazon mechanical turk, and Upwork, to let others design our product for us. The outcome was not spectacular, but it was an interesting way of seeing how these platforms can be used in a design process.
