The illusion of Non-Linearity (PC)

M1.2 - Design Research Project

DRP

MDC

T&R

U&S

C&A

In my previous project, the reporting and dealing with scientific information skills I displayed were severely lacking. This project, I wanted to improve on these areas. The project being a research project made it the perfect opportunity to.

I spent a lot of time this project doing background research, keeping good track of my sources, and writing my report throughout the project, instead of just at the end. Ultimately, I was very proud of what came out of it, even though the research I conducted turned out a bit non-conclusive due to a lack of data.

The data I did have, I analyzed using SPSS statistics. This was the first time I conducted a statistical analysis, and I got to work on these skills so I could later use them again in my FMP, where I was dealing with a lot more data.

The game I made this project stands out among the rest of my work, as it is the only non-educational game I have made. I wanted to broaden my horizon a little, and I really enjoyed trying to make a game that focuses primarily on being fun, working out little details to create a more fun experience, tweaking the mechanics of the game for a good look and feel. The research question I was trying to answer can actually be seen as a “little detail” like this.

I was investigating a concept I came up with called “the illusion of non-linearity”. The idea was to combine an open world game, giving players autonomy over where they go, with loading levels linearly, giving level designers more control over the difficulty of the game.

The difference between the two versions of the game was very small, but I hoped it would create a noticeable difference in the level of autonomy. It just shows how dedicated I am in creating the optimal experience.
